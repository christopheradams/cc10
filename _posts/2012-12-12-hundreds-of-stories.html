Hundreds of people have told us how CC licensing has helped them and their communities. Cost to reuse = $0.

In September 2012, the Obama administration announced the second round of grants in a four-year, two-billion-dollar initiative for community colleges to develop professional training programs and from which all materials will be licensed under CC BY. Anyone, anywhere can reuse them at zero cost.

$0: cost for reusing educational materials developed by #TAACCCT grantees thanks to @creativecommons licensing. 1.usa.gov/S6ZcqQ

— Hilda L. Solis (@HildaSolisDOL) September 19, 2012

Collaboration enables faster content creation.

Collaborative hackathons enable faster content creation and are no longer the domain of coders and geeks. This year, a group of Finnish mathematicians produced a high-quality, CC BY-licensed mathematics textbook in just 48 hours.

Joonas Mäkinen / Senja Opettaa / CC BY

The Libre Bus is a project inspired by Free Culture principles and was arranged by a team of 19 Commons experts. In 2012 many people rode the bus through four South American countries, sharing open knowledge and practices as they went.
