Hundreds of people have told us how CC licensing has helped them and their communities.

 

Free interactive textbooks.

In Poland free digital textbooks will be provided to primary schools as part of a 'Digital Schools' program. The program also aims to create a national repository of training materials.

 

Physical textbook costs cut by 74%.

The South African government has printed and distributed more than 2.4 million physical copies of Siyavula textbooks at nominal cost. Each item costs roughly 40 rand, or 26 percent the cost of a traditional textbook.

 

Cost to reuse = $0.

$0: cost for reusing educational materials developed by #TAACCCT grantees thanks to @creativecommons licensing. 1.usa.gov/S6ZcqQ

— Hilda L. Solis (@HildaSolisDOL) September 19, 2012

 

Hackathons enable faster content creation and are no longer the domain of coders and geeks.

    In 2012 a group of Finnish mathematicians produced a high-quality, CC BY-licensed mathematics textbook in just 48 hours.

Joonas Mäkinen / Senja Opettaa / CC BY

The Libre Bus is a project inspired by Free Culture principles. In 2012, 51 passengers rode the bus 8,000 kilometres during 35 days through Chile, Argentina, Paraguay and Uruguay. They shared open knowledge and practices via content production, talks, workshops and press promotion in more than 40 events. Over 1,000 people in 25 cities were visited by the Librebus.
