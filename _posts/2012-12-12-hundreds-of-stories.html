Hundreds of people have told us how CC licensing has helped them and their communities.

 

Textbooks may be printed and distributed at nominal cost.

In Poland and South Africa students may download textbooks (that include videos and presentations) for free. In Poland free digital textbooks will be provided to primary schools as part of a 'Digital Schools' program. The program also aims to create a national repository of training materials. The South African government has printed and distributed more than 2.4 million physical copies of Siyavula textbooks at nominal cost. Each item costs roughly 40 rand, or 26 percent the cost of a traditional textbook. Both projects use CC licenses. Similar initiatives are underway in British Columbia (Canada) and California (USA).

 

Cost to reuse = $0.

In September 2012, the Obama administration announced the second round of grants in a four-year, two-billion-dollar initiative for community colleges to develop professional training programs and from which all materials will be licensed under CC BY. Anyone, anywhere can reuse them at zero cost.

Collaboration enables faster content creation.

    Collaborative hackathons enable faster content creation and are no longer the domain of coders and geeks. This year, a group of Finnish mathematicians produced a high-quality, CC BY-licensed mathematics textbook in just 48 hours.

Joonas Mäkinen / Senja Opettaa / CC BY

The Libre Bus is a project inspired by Free Culture principles. In 2012, 51 passengers rode the bus 8000 kilometres through Chile, Argentina, Paraguay and Uruguay, stopping to share open knowledge and practices via content production, talks, workshops and press promotion. More than 1,000 people were able to experience Librebus during the 35 days of travel to nearly 25 cities and in more than 40 events.
